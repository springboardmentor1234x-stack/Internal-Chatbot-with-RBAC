financial report for finsolve technologies inc. 2024

executive summary
2024 marked a year of both opportunity and challenge for finsolve technologies. despite a robust revenue increase we saw significant pressure in certain expense categories notably vendor related costs and software subscriptions. however these pressures were balanced by cost saving measures in operational efficiency strong gross margin performance and strategic investment in growth areas. the company is well positioned to continue scaling its core offerings but focused attention on cost optimization will be essential for maintaining profitability in the coming years.

year over year yoy analysis
finsolve technologies revenue grew by 25 percent in 2024 driven largely by the global expansion of its services especially in asia and europe. this was accompanied by a 10 percent increase in vendor related expenses impacting overall profit margins. while gross profit increased by 25 percent reflecting higher operational efficiency net income saw a more modest increase of 12 percent. this suggests that while revenue growth is strong controlling vendor costs and maintaining healthy cash flows remain key to long term profitability.

expense breakdown by category
the primary drivers of expense in 2024 were vendor services totaling 30 million dollars representing an 18 percent increase from the previous year. major components included marketing related expenses accounting for 40 percent training and education expenses accounting for 30 percent software subscriptions accounting for 25 percent and other miscellaneous expenses accounting for 5 percent. dinner and training categories accounted for an increasing share of the marketing budget requiring tighter management.

software subscriptions totaled 25 million dollars up 22 percent from 2023 due to heavy reliance on cloud based tools and saas platforms.

employee benefits and hr costs increased by 10 percent due to growth in headcount.

other operational expenses totaled 15 million dollars including travel and office related costs with an 8 percent year over year increase.

cash flow analysis
cash flow from operations remained positive at 50 million dollars a 20 percent increase from the prior year. delayed vendor payments affected liquidity during the second half of 2024. investments in research development new market entry and acquisitions totaled 15 million dollars. financing activities recorded a net inflow of 5 million dollars for working capital management.
